Tribute to Dr. Manmohan Singh

Dr. Manmohan Singh is a renowned Indian economist and politician who served as the Prime Minister of India from 2004 to 2014. He is widely respected for his role in liberalizing the Indian economy and steering the country towards rapid growth and development.

Known for his humility, intellect, and dedication, Dr. Singh played a pivotal role in shaping modern India's economic policies. His tenure as Finance Minister in the early 1990s marked a turning point in India's economic reforms.

Beyond politics, Dr. Singh is admired for his scholarly contributions and his commitment to public service. His legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians and economists worldwide.

Personal Details

  • Date of Birth: September 26, 1932
  • Place of Birth: Gah, Punjab, British India (now in Pakistan)
  • Spouse: Gursharan Kaur

Awards and Honors

  • Padma Vibhushan (1987) - India's second-highest civilian award
  • Order of the Aztec Eagle (2005) - Mexico's highest honor awarded to foreigners
  • Doctor of Laws (Honoris Causa) from various universities
